### Background

---

We have an ads test with promising results for a service sending an email once a month. When you fail to click it, it generates a prewritten email to a next of kin.

Let’s see if we can create actual conversion based on the data from the test. 

We have the data from the landing page in the test to beat.

Let’s experiment. 

#### Possible Reasons For Low Conversion Previously

---

Here are some of the potential culprits to the ordeal that I can think about 👇

- The previous ads are good at gathering attention and curiosity. But not buyer’s intent.
    - This might be because of setup: change ads from “landing pages views” to “conversions”
    - This will require that we install a meta pexel situation — which I have no idea how to do. But we will figure it out

### What makes this project a success?

---

We have gathered more learning about what can be the cause for the conversion draught. And we can see if we can verify the theory that an ads test will give more useful data for converting customers. 

We get a bigger understanding of actually how much work is required to create said landing page.

#### Must

---

- Have a landing page that track clicks as indication for purchase intent so we can compare the data

#### Should

---

- Have tested with a meta pexel setup
- Be mobile friendly - seems like most of the traffic is mobile
- If we can use a subdomain under [blackdotads.com](http://blackdotads.com) for the test - this is cheaper. E.g: [https://alive.blackdotads.com](https://alive.blackdotads.com/)

#### Could

---

- Compare performance for pexel ads vs traffic ads
- Have other measurements
- Use discounts as a way to incentivise people to sign up

### Out of scope

---

We are creating a click test. So we don’t need to hook the payment up to real payments. And we don’t need to make the actual email sender service.

### Suggested Page Structure

---

Landing page w “get started” and “buy” under buttons w pricing that could make a business case. 

Hypothesis: $4/month and $40/year — The service doesn’t require much cost as far as I can see. But tell me if you see any expenses that I can’t see. There is no point in testing a price point that can’t be sustainable. 

But note that Stripe will take 3,5% of all processed payments. 

When people click any of the actionable buttons it can lead to a page with text similar to: 

“We are currently capping membership to ensure maximum system stability. Enter your email to get notified the second a slot opens up.”

Note that I’ve not spent a lot of time on the copy currently, because I have no data. But I’m happy to help out and spar with you if you want to.
